✅ CoAIleague — Workforce Intelligence System Architecture (Organized Specification)

1. Core Concept

CoAIleague has two employee data pools:

A. Organization Employee Pool (Internal Employees)

Each organization has its own private employee list

Employees cannot appear in another organization’s private pool

Standard W2 or internal staff

Used for normal scheduling, shift coverage, recurring roles, full-time/part-time roles


B. Contractor Pool (Cross-Organization)

Pool of 1099 freelancers, gig workers, or specialized seasonal staff

Shared across organizations, but:

Only by category

Only by skillset / certifications / licenses / traits required


Contractors can receive job offers from multiple organizations if:

They meet the job category

They meet all required skills/criteria

They have availability



These two pools are separate, but the AI Brain can pull from either when scheduling.

✅ 4. AI Scheduling Algorithm (High-Level Flow)

Step 1 — Identify job requirements

AI reads job properties (skills, certs, etc.)

Step 2 — Search Internal Pool (W2)

Query:

SELECT employees
WHERE organization_id = job.organization_id
AND skills match
AND certifications match
AND availability matches
ORDER BY priority rules

If match found → assign & stop.

Step 3 — Search Contractor Pool

Query:

SELECT contractor
WHERE categories include job.category
AND skills match
AND certifications match
AND availability matches

If matches → send offer to top-ranked contractors.

Step 4 — Bidding / Acceptance

Contractors can:

Accept

Decline

Ignore (timer auto-expires)


Step 5 — Assign

If accepted → assign the contractor.

If not → go to next contractor.
